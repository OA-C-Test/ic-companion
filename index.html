Interstitial Cystitis Companion

Private questionnaires to organize your bladder health — and dig deeper, at your own pace.

Pick a questionnaire below. Each one is private, saves on your device as you go, and turns your answers into a clear summary you can keep, share with a clinician, or explore further with an AI assistant.

This tool helps you organize information. It is not a diagnosis and does not replace medical care or testing.

Privacy & sharing

You decide what is shared and what stays yours.

In short: Answer everything honestly. If there's an answer you'd rather not share, tap Keep private to hide just that one from the copy you share. Nothing leaves this device unless you copy it and send it yourself.

Keeping answers private

Every question has a Keep private toggle. Tap it on any answer you'd rather not share. You can still answer openly — private answers stay in your own copy.

Two copies when you finish

Shareable copy

Share this with a doctor, a family member, or anyone you choose. Anything you marked private appears only as [Kept private] — the actual content is removed, so it cannot be read.

Your private copy

For you only. It includes every answer, including the private ones. Keep it somewhere private, like your Notes app.

How the privacy actually works

Privacy here means withholding, not secret codes. A private answer is simply left out of the shareable copy — it is never placed into what you send, so there is nothing for anyone to decode later. In a copy-and-paste setup, this is more honest and dependable than encryption.

Continuing with an AI (optional)

If you're comfortable sharing with an AI

Your private copy is yours to explore. If you feel comfortable putting health details into an AI assistant, you can open a fresh AI chat — or one of your own Claude Projects — paste your copy in, and ask your own questions, such as:

"Based on these answers, what should I ask my urologist?"
"In plain language, what might this pattern suggest — and what does it not prove?"
"Help me turn this into a weekly symptom diary I can update."

Sharing health information with any AI is entirely your choice. Share only what you're comfortable putting into a chat. An AI can help you learn and prepare, but it does not diagnose or treat — for sensitive or urgent concerns, a clinician is the right place.

What this is

What it's for

    Important

    This is not a diagnosis. IC/BPS is identified by clinicians after other causes (such as infection) are ruled out. This tool organizes information — it does not replace medical care or testing.

    How it works — step by step

    1. Answer each question by tapping your choice. The box turns green when it's picked.
    2. Would you rather keep an answer to yourself? Tap Keep private on that question.
    3. Your answers save on this device automatically — you can stop and come back any time.
    4. When you're done, tap See my results at the bottom.
    5. You'll get two copies: one to share (with private answers hidden) and a private one just for you.
    6. Copy or send whichever you like — or simply keep it for your records.
    Tip: To make the words bigger, put two fingers on the screen and gently spread them apart (on a computer, hold Ctrl — or ⌘ on a Mac — and press +). To copy your results, tap the Copy button, then paste them into a text, email, or notes.

    The questions

    Tap your answer to each question — it turns green when picked. Tap Keep private on any question to hide that answer from the copy you share. Everything saves automatically; tap See my results when you're done.

    Start a new chat

    1. Tap Open a new chat below. If you have the Claude app, it opens; otherwise the website opens.
    2. Sign in, or create a free account if you don't have one yet.
    3. Tap the message box, then paste (press and hold, then choose Paste).
    4. Send the message. Claude will use what you pasted to help you.

    Or add it to one of your Projects

    Projects keep related chats and notes in one place — handy if you want all your summaries together over time.

    1. Tap Open my Projects below.
    2. Open an existing project, or create one (for example, "My bladder health").
    3. Start a chat inside the project, tap the message box, and paste.
    4. Send — or add it to the project's knowledge so Claude remembers it for future chats.
